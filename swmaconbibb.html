Recycling Project Breakdown

To better explain how I went about creating this project I will be referencing my process throughout the breakdown

Step 1: Discovery

My use of the word DISCOVERY is separated from RESEARCH in my user experience process. During discovery I identify problems or discover new solutions for governments and agencies. I do this in order to establish a scope of the project and what will be focused on in the research phase.

The Macon-Bibb County Government decided to rollout a new recycling program for its citizens to help improve their quality of life and maintain a clean and green city. My team was tasked with educating the citizens about recycling and getting individuals enrolled for this new service. During my team meetings we came to the conclusion that the best solution would be to combine the education campaign with the enrollment process.

My role was to research and develop the platform to bridge everything together.

Step 2: Research

Once we identify the problem that needs to be addressed, or a new idea needing to be explored, I begin my process of RESEARCH. I begin by seeing what the best standards for the project may be, and then look at what other contemporary organizations have created so I can establish a basis and innovate from there. For the recycling program, I needed to do some digging into what was currently being done by other governmental authorities, specifically in regards to recycling initiatives. It was important to see if they had any pre-developed solutions we could utilize or build upon.

Step 3: Design

Once we established our basis for working and defined the scope of what we are looking to create, I began to DESIGN. I built mock ups and rapid prototypes to give a visual presence to our ideas.

Step 4: Review

After the design process is complete, we send in the current versions for REVIEW to all parties involved. We discuss the design to ensure that it embodies their overall message and meets the goals set in the initial discovery phase.

Step 5: Debug

We then take the user-testing data and stakeholder information and begin to DEBUG any issues they may have been discovered. Then, we prepare the final product for release.

Step 6: Render

After completing the review and debug process, we RENDER out the final product and begin releasing it to the community (via the appropriate media channels) so everyone has the opportunity to benefit from the new service.
