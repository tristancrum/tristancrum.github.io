MY UX PROCESS

DISCOVERY

My use of the word DISCOVERY is separated from RESEARCH in my user experience process. During discovery I identify problems or discover new solutions for governments and agencies. I do this in order to establish a scope of the project and what will be focused on in the research phase.

RESEARCH

Once we identify the problem that needs to be addressed, or a new idea needing to be explored, I begin my process of RESEARCH. I begin by seeing what the best standards for the project may be, and then look at what other contemporary organizations have created so I can establish a basis and innovate from there.

DESIGN

Once we established our basis for working and defined the scope of what we are looking to create, I began to DESIGN. I then begin to build mock-ups and higher fidelity wire frames and rapid prototypes to give a visual presence to the solution or ideas.

REVIEW

After the design process is complete, we send in the current versions for REVIEW to all parties involved. We discuss the design to ensure that it embodies their overall message and meets the goals set in the initial discovery phase.

DEBUG

We then take the user-testing data and stakeholder information and begin to DEBUG any issues they may have been discovered. Then, we prepare the final product for release.

RENDER

After completing the review and debug process, we RENDER out the final product and begin releasing it to the community (via the appropriate media channels) so everyone has the opportunity to benefit from the new service.
